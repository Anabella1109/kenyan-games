KENYA

Kibe

Koi/Kibe-is one of the most popular children’s game among the Swahili people of the Kenyan coast. It is a “hide and seek” game for children for children between 5 and 12 yrs. Around ten children gather at the village playground and one is chosen to be the explorer. He/she then shuts his/her eyes and counts out the numbers 1-10 loudly. By the time he/she reaches count ten and opens the eyes, everyone is supposed to have found a place to hide. The explorer is then supposed to start seeking them out. The kid who hides longest without being discovered is awarded a prize commonly referred to as “gold medal” and the game continues with the one who was discovered first becoming the next explorer.

Rukongoro

Rukongoro-is a traditional Ameru board game but is also common among the Tigania, Imenti, and Igembe of Mt.Kenya region. It takes two to tango in this game that do not a mass from your side. The other rule is that do not use bad or foul language against opponent. This game promotes inter-community cohesion and resilience

Enkeshui

Enkeshui-is a traditional board game played only by Maasai men both Kenya and Tanzania. Enkeshui can be play using mancala board of different sizes, as long as they have two rows of pits. The number of pits in each row may vary; it is usually 8, 10 or 12. 48 seeds are used. As for many traditional mancala games, it is unclear whether the initial setup is fixed or if it may be chosen by an agreement between the players. To choose which player will move first, an initial "sowing race" takes place. Both players take all the seeds from one of their pits and relay-sow them concurrently. The first player who finishes sowing will be the first to play in the remainder of the game. Notice that since the initial race is concurrent, its outcome is quite unpredictable. Thus, each game will actually begin (after the race) with a different initial setup. Game ends when one of the players cannot move anymore, because he has no seeds in his row or because he only has seeds in bull pits. The opponent then captures all the seeds in his row, except for that in bull pits; seeds in bull pits will be captured by the player owning the bull. The player who captured most seeds wins the game. The game teaches on banking rules of banking and withdrawal.
